What is this game about?

A village in the Peruvian Andes, plagued by demons who walk among its people — and the demons are you and your friends. Everyone gets a hidden role, votes are cast in secret, and nobody is ever quite sure who to trust. A round takes about 25 minutes.

Ayakucho has a predecessor: Bamberg, born during the Covid years and sold to a British publisher in 2022. Seeing its potential unfulfilled, Wilfredo bought the license back in 2023, partnered up with Maryia, and started over. Three years of design and testing around Europe later, the game is finally ready.

How is this different from Werewolf, Blood on the Clocktower or Secret Hitler?

Three things. Anonymous voting: nobody sees who condemned whom, and that changes everything about how you bluff. Quick rounds: about 25 minutes each. And you don't need a crowd: the basic mode plays with just six, while the second mode — for 8 to 12 players — splits the table into three teams, a favorite among social deduction veterans.
